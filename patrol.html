Patrol Logger

Audit the guard regime by logging it. Set the checkpoints, then tap a station each time a patrol passes it. Patrol times the intervals and shows where the regime is weak: a cadence regular enough to be timed and avoided, gaps that leave a station unwatched, and checkpoints the patrol never reaches. The weaknesses raise as findings for the report.

What to watch

Predictable cadence is the headline weakness. A patrol that hits a checkpoint on a fixed interval can be timed, so an adversary simply waits out the pass. Low variation between intervals is the signal, and the fix is to randomize timing and route.
Coverage gaps leave a station unwatched. A long stretch between passes, or a checkpoint the patrol never reaches, is an opening. Look for the longest gap against the mean.
Route order can be learned the same way timing is. If the patrol always walks the same sequence, the time to any one point is predictable from the time at the last.
Watch how patrols respond to a trigger as well, like a propped door or an alarm, since a regime that does not break its loop to investigate is a weakness in itself.

Logging honestly

Log only what you observe during an authorized assessment, and note the observation window so the statistics are read in context. A short window can make an irregular patrol look regular or the reverse.
Capture supporting proof in Exhibit and record the confirmed weaknesses as findings here, which carry into Docket with the rest of the assessment.

What Flux detects

Magnetic locks (maglocks), which hold with a strong permanent or electromagnetic field at the door header.
Door and window contacts, where a reed switch is held by a small magnet on the moving leaf.
Concealed ferrous hardware: strike plates, reinforcing, hinges, fasteners, and rebar behind a surface.
Speakers, motors, and transformers, which also carry strong fields and can be mistaken for hardware.

How to read it

The ambient earth field runs roughly 25 to 65 microtesla. Calibrate against it with the device held still and away from metal.
Sweep slowly and watch the deviation, not the raw number. A door contact magnet nudges it; a maglock pins it.
Sensors vary by device and drift with temperature and orientation. Recalibrate if the baseline wanders.
Flux identifies the presence of a field. Record what it means as a finding in Sweep and the proof in Exhibit.

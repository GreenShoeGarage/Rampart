Reading the meter

The level is a relative brightness index, not calibrated lux. Phone cameras auto-expose, so use Lumen to compare spots and to judge evenness, not to certify an absolute light level.
Uniformity is the honest signal. Auto-exposure shifts the whole frame together, so the spread between the darkest and brightest cell still tells you where the dark pockets are.
Hold the camera level and face the area the way an approaching person would see it.

What good lighting looks like

Even coverage matters more than raw brightness. A bright pool next to a black gap is worse than a moderate, uniform wash.
Watch for dark pockets at approaches, entries, and along the perimeter line, and for glare that blinds rather than reveals.
Light and natural surveillance work together: lighting only helps if someone or a camera can see the lit space.
Record the weak spots as findings in Sweep and capture the proof in Exhibit.
